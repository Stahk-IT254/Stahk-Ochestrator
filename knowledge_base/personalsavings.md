# SECTION: Personal Savings Fundamentals

## Summary
Saving money involves setting clear goals, controlling expenses, and consistently allocating income toward future needs. A structured savings plan increases financial stability and reduces risk during emergencies.

## Types of Savings Goals
- Short-term (0–6 months): emergencies, small purchases
- Medium-term (6–24 months): education, travel, business startup
- Long-term (2+ years): home ownership, retirement

## Core Saving Principles
- Pay yourself first (save before spending)
- Track income and expenses
- Set a fixed savings percentage (10–30%)
- Build consistency over time

## Common Savings Methods
- Fixed monthly savings
- Percentage-based savings
- Goal-based saving (target amount)

## Example
If income = KES 50,000:
- Save 20% → KES 10,000/month
- Remaining → expenses and discretionary spending

## Challenges
- Irregular income
- Unexpected expenses
- Lack of discipline
